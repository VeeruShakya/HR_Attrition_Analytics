# Corporate HR Analytics & Employee Attrition Dashboard 📉

## 📌 Executive Summary
Business owners lose millions of dollars every year replacing staff because they rely on gut feelings instead of data to predict turnover. 

This project features an end-to-end HR Analytics pipeline and an interactive Power BI dashboard designed to diagnose employee attrition. By analyzing 1,470 employee records, this tool isolates specific flight risks across departments, salary brackets, and demographics, allowing HR teams to proactively improve retention.

## 🎯 The Business Problem
A company cannot fix its culture or retention rates if it doesn't know exactly where the cracks are. Common HR blind spots include:
* Not knowing which age demographics are churning the fastest.
* Assuming turnover is only tied to low salaries, while ignoring high-skill role burnout.
* Lacking visibility into the critical "Years at Company" milestones where employees are most likely to exit.

## 🛠️ The Analytics Architecture
* **Data Ingestion & Cleaning:** Standardized a raw dataset of 1,470 employee records, ensuring data integrity across demographic and financial columns.
* **Data Modeling:** Built a robust relational model to support seamless cross-filtering.
* **DAX Engineering:** Engineered dynamic measures for Attrition Count, Attrition Rate, Average Salary, and Average Tenure.
* **Visualization:** Designed an interactive executive dashboard highlighting key vulnerability points in the workforce.

## 📊 Key Business Insights Discovered
* **The 16% Bleed:** The overall company attrition rate sits at an unhealthy 16.12% (237 exits out of 1,470 employees).
* **The Flight-Risk Demographic:** Employees aged 26-35 are churning at the absolute highest rate (116 exits), indicating a failure to retain mid-level talent.
* **The Salary Floor:** 163 employees exited from the lowest salary bracket (Up to 5k). 
* **Role-Specific Burnout:** Highly skilled, specialized roles like Laboratory Technicians (62 exits) and Sales Executives (57 exits) are leaving at alarming rates, regardless of the baseline salary data.
* **The First-Year Churn:** The "Attrition by Years at Company" metric spikes massively in the first 1-2 years, indicating a severe flaw in the company's onboarding or initial hiring alignment.
